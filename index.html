Ye Ji (纪野)

Ye Ji is a third-year Ph.D. candidate at Dalian University of Technology, supervised by Prof. Chungang Zhu. He is working in the interdisciplinary field of Computational Science and Engineering (CSE), which brings together applied mathematics, computer sciences, and engineering disciplines to solve challenging real-world problems. Ye's research interests focus on Isogeometric Analysis with particular emphasis on analysis-suitable domain parameterizations. In addition, Ye is a reviewer for Mathematical Reviews and a developer of Geometry + Simulation Modules (G+Smo) which is the biggest open-source software for isogeometric analysis written by C++.

Now, he is conducting a two-year visiting Ph.D. project at Delft University of Technology, supervised by Dr. Matthias Möller. This project mainly focuses on flux-aligned mesh generation for twin-screw machines that are widely used in industry to produce high-pressure air and gases, e.g., for large industrial applications such as chillers, or to operate high-power air tools such as jackhammers and impact wrenches. Further applications of screw machines are in the food and beverage industry, the automotive industry, the chemical and pharmaceutical industry, and industrial manufacturing to name just a few.

    • Research

    Isogeometric analysis (IGA), first introduced by T.J.R. Hughes et al., is a numerical technique that aims to bridge the gap between computer-aided design (CAD) and finite element analysis (FEA). I'm interested in developing the theory and efficient methods of spline-based parameterization, which is a fundamental step in IGA and has a great effect on the subsequent analysis.
